One sticky ball sweat morn in July,
half up a hill of damp and matted grass,
a fire ant mounts on Sisyphus’s knuckle bone,
and wakes him. Jerk-reaching towards his stone,
he only finds a half-mud slick, small roots,
and pebbles. He slits his eyes to scan
the low-hung clouds for signs of Jovian tricks
and sniffs for brimstone-pomegranate scent,
but finds himself alone and out of view.

Unburdened, he climbs the hill with ease and stops
upon its crest. The boulder usually crushes back
and drags him down from here. He stops to pick
a scab before continuing o’er and down
the side he’s never seen. He tucks his knees
against his chest and rolls like hell on wheels
into a greenlush valley’s brook-fed pool.
The water soothes his heat and lifts the dirt
which crusts his dented nail beds, freeing clots
and loosing slicks of blood between his toes.
He lays there, head against the moss, and drifts
into a murk of fuddled freed deranged desire.

“Are there hylics? Likely so. Is’t right
to profit off the vice of hylics, given
that it’s serving higher aims? But no:
to profit using sickness is a greater
sickness still. And whence to find or make
these higher aims? Am I fit to find such
higher aims? What is worth building?
Is the time more fit for building or discovery?
What is worth discovery? What is proper aim?
I think I still care more about increasing
my capabilities. No, that’s not it- no sense
in maximizing maximization. Prefer
embodied physical truth. Avoid mediated
life, it’s not a life at all. Is increased wealth really it?
No, money isn’t agency. Capability is a function
of means, skill and talent, will and context.
I sense that there are many who can still
be saved. Imperative to lift these while
we can. I want to yield a flourishing Man…"

He lifts his arm and puts his teeth against
the crook. He tongues his bulging cubital vein
to test the tautly filamented web
‘fore tearing in, incisors first. He splits
the vein and tastes the collagen notes and blood,
then does the same to adjoining cephalic vein.
Those done, he scrapes through fascia, meets
the long head bicep tendon, rips the cord
full off the bone, then folds it up away,
exposing brachialis, rips again,
adjusts his mouth, severs pronator teres,
his medial epicondyle, triceps, radii,
and other sordid layers of sinew-muscle,
and finally finds the joint itself. He gnaws
the ligaments ‘till they give enough to show
articular cartilage. He puts his wrist between
his knees, applying traction, gradually tears
away the joint, and adjusts his tension pull
to show more capsule, his maxillaries bared
and stripping all the while. With one last heave,
he separates the radius and ulna from the humerus
and what viscera still remains between. It’s done.

Old Noah sits to drink. He’s sunbeat, dry,
and showers down alluvium while loosing
his sandal straps and belt. The silt and sand
that waves and currents scattered round now coat
his sackcloth shirt and grind between his toes.
This land was razed and emptied, purposed for
his seed, but not for him. He shifts his weight
But can’t quite crack his battered hips and back.

The grapes adjusted quickly. Their spreading
roots thrive amid the flood-smoothed rocks strewn o’er
the arid ground. The brilliant verdant green
against the brown and gray of dirt and stone
provide a solace only rivaled by
the fragrant sour wine he swigs, alone
and quiet, nightly under stars and sky.
He rubs his feet together like a locust
to feel the sagged skin’s layers skip and slide.
He shuts his eyes and day’s last sun-warmed breeze
blows past his ears and rustles in his beard.

His stupored dreams: submerged and darkened mounts
are pushed by whirlpools, swirling flow and tides.
In murky blueish dark they grate and fracture
like molars, layering dust to nourish vines.
A fissure rattles open, bellows smoke,
and shoots infernal arms of cooling fire
up towards a spinning shadow near the sun.
They hit the blighted boat and jerk him up,
from drowned and shrouded reverie’s shifting forms.
He trips to bed and, fumbling, shucks his clothes.

Come morn, he wakes still further from Eden
and wonders when his sons will visit.
